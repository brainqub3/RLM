## Core Field Mechanics

### **GBFC (Gravitational Basin of Field Collapse)**
The universal attractor state in a system—the default destination for all trajectories unless intercepted by structured resistance. In the Janus Sequence, this is the (1,1) attractor. In consciousness systems, this represents default behavioral patterns or "lowest energy states."

**Example:** "All unanchored motifs eventually decay toward the GBFC unless stabilized by resonance."

---

### **CPG (Convergence Pressure Gradient)**
A quantitative measure of the decay force pulling a system toward its attractor basin. Low CPG indicates orbital stability; high CPG indicates rapid collapse.

**Formula (simplified):**
```
CPG = (1/k) * Σ(Pₙ) for n in 1 to k
where Pₙ = (Σⁿ - Δⁿ) / Σⁿ₋₁
```

**Range:** 0.0 (maximum stability) to 1.0+ (rapid collapse)

---

### **Δ-Stability (Delta Stability)**
The hysteresis buffer measuring resistance to state change. High Δ-Stability indicates a system maintains coherence despite perturbations.

**Application:** Used to identify "tide pool" attractors—temporary stable states within larger convergence fields.
